The Spirit

In the enchanting land of Malaysia, where history and nature intertwine, RAKSASA emerges as a legendary spirit — a charismatic giant, the life of every party, and the storyteller that turns gatherings into unforgettable adventures.

Conceived by the imaginative minds at Cave & Cellar, RAKSASA is crafted in a century-old distillery using traditional methods and locally sourced ingredients. At its heart is smooth rice wine, blended with vibrant botanicals to produce a carnival of flavours in every sip.

This is not just a drink. It is bold, potent and steeped in tradition — a spirit that carries the soul of a place and the thrill of a celebration, bottled for those who know that the best stories are shared.

The Mythical Fusion

Where ancient myth meets modern sophistication

Our identity draws on two powerful mythological creatures — the Raksasa and the Nian — merging their legendary essence into one bold spirit.

The Majestic Beast

RAKSASA

In Southeast Asian mythology, the Raksasa is a formidable creature — revered and feared for its strength and mystical powers. Symbolising might, protection and resilience, it embodies the spirit of our drinks: bold, potent and steeped in tradition.

The Symbol of Renewal

Nian

A creature of Chinese legend, the Nian emerges every Lunar New Year — warding off evil and heralding new beginnings. It represents renewal, the cyclical nature of life, and our commitment to blending ancient tradition with contemporary craft.

Joo Lee Chan

A heritage of tradition & authenticity

The origins of RAKSASA trace back to the humble town of Mantin in the early 1900s. Arriving from China, Ong Eng Suan of the Tai Yuan Clan brought with him the secrets of his village — the ancient art of wine-making.

1906

The first distillery, Mantin

With a handful of skilled craftsmen, a modest distillery was established. Soon the local air was filled with the rich aroma of mijiu — rice wine.

1920s

A move to Seremban

After the First World War, the thriving enterprise relocated. Through the ages, the distillation techniques and natural alchemy remained unchanged.

Today

An unbroken legacy

Joo Lee Chan continues to honour its founder by crafting premium mijiu — steeped in centuries of tradition, exactly as Ong envisioned.

Signature Serves

Recipes worth gathering for

Seven serves devised for the seven spirits. Tap any card to reveal the full recipe.

01
Original Gin

The Genmai

Genmaicha-infused gin, lemon & apple soda.

Genmaicha-infused Original Gin30 ml
Lemon juice1 wedge
Apple soda90 ml

Infuse gin with genmaicha tea (1:50) in a sealed jar for one day. Build over cubed ice — lemon, infused gin — then top with apple soda. Garnish with sliced lemon.

02
Nutmeg Gin

Spiced Beast

Lime, spiced syrup & a whisper of soda.

Nutmeg Gin50 ml
Lime juice15 ml
Spiced syrup10 ml
Soda30 ml

Shake gin, lime and spiced syrup with ice. Strain into a chilled glass and top with soda. Garnish with sliced nutmeg.

Recipe +
03
Hibiscus Gin

Bloom & Longan

Chrysanthemum, longan, cucumber & soda.

Infused Hibiscus Gin30 ml
Soda90 ml
Cucumber, long-sliced1 pc

Infuse 150 g gin with 2 g dried chrysanthemum and 10 g dried longan for one day. Line the glass with cucumber, fill with ice, build the infusion and top with soda. Stir before drinking.

Recipe +
04
Sitrus Gin

Earl of Calamansi

Earl grey, asam boi & calamansi.

Sitrus Gin45 ml
Earl grey tea60 ml
Asam boi1 pc
Calamansi juice10 ml
Syrup10 ml
Soda30 ml

Steep earl grey (1:50) for 3 minutes. Shake asam boi, syrup, calamansi, tea and gin with ice. Strain over cubed ice, top with soda, garnish with mint.

Gula Melaka Rum

Tropic Colada

Mango, coconut cream & lime.

Gula Melaka Rum45 ml
Mango juice60 ml
Syrup10 ml
Lime juice15 ml
Coconut cream30 ml

Shake all ingredients hard with ice. Strain into a chilled glass and garnish with coconut flakes.
